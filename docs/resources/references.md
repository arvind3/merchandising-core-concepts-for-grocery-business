# References

This book is grounded in common retail operating practices and established merchandising frameworks used across supermarket, mass retail, and CPG collaboration models.

## Industry and Professional Sources

1. Category management frameworks used by leading grocery and CPG organizations.
2. Retail pricing and promotion practices from supermarket operator playbooks.
3. Grocery replenishment and inventory governance methods used in store and DC operations.
4. Supplier performance governance standards including OTIF and fill-rate metrics.
5. Retail analytics methods for basket affinity, elasticity, and event post-analysis.

## Data Domains Referenced in Chapter Examples

- POS transaction history
- Item master and product hierarchy
- Planogram files and shelf-capacity data
- Price and promotion calendars
- Purchase order, lead-time, and supplier service data
- Store audit and compliance records
- Loyalty and trip-level basket data
- External context signals such as seasonality and weather

## Notes on Example Data

- Chapter scenarios use representative, illustrative figures designed to explain decision logic.
- Financial and operational metrics are presented in realistic ranges for learning purposes, not as company-specific disclosures.
- Interpretation sections focus on managerial decisions and tradeoffs rather than vendor-specific tooling.
