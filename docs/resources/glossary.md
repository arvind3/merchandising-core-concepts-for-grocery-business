# Glossary

Common terms and definitions used throughout this book.

## A

**API (Application Programming Interface)**
A set of protocols and tools for building software applications. Enables different systems to communicate with each other.

**Assortment**
The selection of products offered in a category or store.

## B

**BOGO (Buy One Get One)**
A promotional offer where customers receive a free item when purchasing one at regular price.

**BOPIS (Buy Online, Pick Up In Store)**
Omnichannel fulfillment option allowing customers to order online and collect at a physical store.

**Buyer**
A role responsible for supplier negotiations, purchase orders, and supplier relationship management.

## C

**Category Management**
The process of managing product categories as strategic business units to maximize sales and profitability.

**Category Manager**
The person responsible for a specific product category's performance, assortment, pricing, and promotions.

**Cold Chain**
Temperature-controlled supply chain for perishable products.

**CPFR (Collaborative Planning, Forecasting, and Replenishment)**
A business practice where retailers and suppliers share information to improve supply chain efficiency.

## D

**DC (Distribution Center)**
A warehouse facility where products are received, stored, and shipped to stores.

**Demand Forecasting**
The process of predicting future customer demand to optimize inventory and supply.

**Distribution**
The movement of products from suppliers through distribution centers to stores.

## E

**EDI (Electronic Data Interchange)**
Standardized electronic communication for business documents like purchase orders and invoices.

**EDLP (Everyday Low Price)**
A pricing strategy offering consistently low prices without frequent promotions.

**EOQ (Economic Order Quantity)**
The optimal order quantity that minimizes total inventory costs.

**ERP (Enterprise Resource Planning)**
Integrated software system for managing core business processes.

**ESB (Enterprise Service Bus)**
Middleware architecture for connecting and integrating enterprise applications.

## F

**Fill Rate**
The percentage of customer orders that can be fulfilled from available inventory.

**Facing**
The practice of arranging products at the front of shelves for a neat appearance.

## G

**Gross Margin**
The difference between revenue and cost of goods sold (COGS), expressed as a percentage.

**Gross Margin ROI (GMROI)**
A profitability metric measuring return on inventory investment.

## H

**High-Low Pricing**
A pricing strategy with regular higher prices and frequent promotional discounts.

## I

**Inventory Turns**
The number of times inventory is sold and replaced over a period. Calculated as COGS / Average Inventory.

**IoT (Internet of Things)**
Network of physical devices with sensors and connectivity for data exchange.

## K

**KPI (Key Performance Indicator)**
A measurable value that demonstrates how effectively objectives are being achieved.

## L

**Lead Time**
The time between placing an order and receiving it.

**Loyalty Program**
A rewards program that incentivizes repeat customer purchases.

## M

**Markdown**
A price reduction to clear inventory, typically for seasonal or expiring products.

**Merchandising**
The strategic planning, execution, and management of product offerings to maximize sales and customer satisfaction.

**MMS (Merchandise Management System)**
Central system for managing product information, pricing, and promotions.

**MOQ (Minimum Order Quantity)**
The smallest quantity a supplier will accept for an order.

**Multi-buy**
A promotional offer providing discounts when purchasing multiple units (e.g., "3 for $5").

## O

**Omnichannel**
An integrated approach providing seamless shopping experiences across all channels (in-store, online, mobile).

**Out-of-Stock (OOS)**
A situation where a product is unavailable for purchase.

## P

**Planogram**
A visual diagram showing the exact placement of products on store shelves.

**POS (Point of Sale)**
The system and location where customer transactions are completed.

**Price Elasticity**
A measure of how demand changes in response to price changes.

**Pricing Strategy**
The approach used to set prices to achieve business objectives.

**Procurement**
The process of acquiring goods and services from external sources.

**Product Hierarchy**
The structured organization of products from broad categories to individual SKUs.

**Promotion Management**
The planning, execution, and analysis of temporary price reductions or special offers.

## Q

**QE (Quality Engineering)**
The practice of ensuring systems and processes meet defined quality standards through systematic testing.

**Quality Engineer**
A professional responsible for testing systems, validating data, and ensuring quality standards.

## R

**Reorder Point (ROP)**
The inventory level that triggers a new order.

**Replenishment**
The process of restocking inventory to maintain optimal levels.

**RFID (Radio Frequency Identification)**
Technology using radio waves to track inventory and products.

## S

**Safety Stock**
Extra inventory held to protect against variability in demand or supply.

**SKU (Stock Keeping Unit)**
A unique identifier for each distinct product item.

**Shrink**
Inventory loss due to theft, damage, or errors.

**Supplier Management**
Activities related to selecting, contracting, monitoring, and developing supplier relationships.

## T

**TCO (Total Cost of Ownership)**
The purchase price plus all associated costs of a product or system.

**TPR (Temporary Price Reduction)**
A short-term discount on the regular price.

## W

**WMS (Warehouse Management System)**
Software for managing warehouse operations including receiving, storage, and shipping.
