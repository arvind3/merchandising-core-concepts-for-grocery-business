# Glossary

## Basket Analytics
Analysis of item combinations purchased within the same transaction to identify affinity, substitution, and trip-mission patterns.

## Category Management
A retail management discipline that treats a product category as a strategic business unit with defined growth, margin, and shopper objectives.

## Category Role
Strategic purpose assigned to a category, commonly traffic builder, transaction builder, profit generator, or image enhancer.

## EDLP (Everyday Low Price)
Pricing strategy focused on stable competitive prices rather than frequent deep promotions.

## Elasticity
The degree to which shopper demand changes in response to price change.

## Facings
The number of visible units of a SKU presented on shelf.

## Fill Rate
Percentage of ordered quantity delivered by a supplier or distribution node.

## GMROI (Gross Margin Return on Inventory Investment)
A profitability metric calculated as gross margin dollars divided by average inventory cost.

## KVI (Key Value Item)
A highly visible item that strongly influences shopper perception of a retailer's price competitiveness.

## OSA (On-Shelf Availability)
Percentage of time a product is available for purchase on shelf when shoppers are ready to buy.

## OTIF (On Time In Full)
Supplier service metric indicating the percentage of shipments delivered on time and with complete quantity.

## Planogram
A visual and data-driven plan that defines shelf placement, facings, and adjacency rules for items in a category.

## Promo ROI
Return on investment from promotional spending, often measured as incremental gross profit divided by discount and execution cost.

## Reorder Point
Inventory threshold that triggers replenishment, typically demand during lead time plus safety stock.

## Safety Stock
Buffer inventory maintained to absorb uncertainty in demand and lead time.

## Shrink
Inventory loss from spoilage, damage, theft, or administrative error.

## Store Cluster
A grouping of stores with similar demand behavior, demographics, and operating conditions used for policy segmentation.

## Turns (Inventory Turns)
How many times inventory is sold and replaced over a period; a measure of inventory velocity and capital productivity.
