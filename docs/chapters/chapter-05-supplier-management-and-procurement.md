# Chapter 5: Supplier Management and Procurement

Supplier strategy in grocery is operational risk management with commercial consequences. Procurement quality determines product cost, in-stock reliability, freshness, and ultimately customer trust.

## 1. Concept Explanation

A mature supplier operating model includes:

1. **Sourcing strategy** by category (single source, dual source, regional source).
2. **Commercial negotiation** (cost, funding, rebates, payment terms).
3. **Service agreements** (lead time, fill rate, OTIF, quality compliance).
4. **Joint business planning** (new items, events, seasonal readiness).
5. **Supplier scorecards** tied to corrective action.

For grocery, service performance can be as economically important as unit cost.

## 2. Why It Matters to the Business

| Supplier lever | Business effect |
|---|---|
| Better net cost | Margin expansion |
| Higher OTIF | Fewer stockouts and emergency logistics |
| Tighter quality controls | Lower shrink and claims |
| Joint planning discipline | More predictable promotion execution |

Low-cost suppliers with weak service often create hidden costs that exceed negotiated savings.

## 3. Example Scenario: Private Label Reliability Program

A grocery retailer expanded private label across pantry and chilled categories but struggled with service volatility from several mid-sized manufacturers.

### Program Actions

- Supplier segmentation into strategic, core, and tactical tiers.
- Contract refresh: OTIF penalties and service recovery actions.
- Monthly joint planning cadence for high-velocity SKUs.
- Early warning dashboard for lead-time deviation and fill-rate decline.

### Result After Three Quarters (Illustrative)

| KPI | Before | After |
|---|---:|---:|
| OTIF | 84% | 94% |
| Fill rate on promo items | 87% | 96% |
| Inbound quality claims | 100 index | 71 index |
| Private-label sales growth | 5.2% | 9.8% |

## 4. Data Interpretation

The analysis split supplier performance into cost and service dimensions. Several suppliers with attractive costs showed chronic lead-time instability and poor case-level quality. Once service-adjusted total cost was calculated, sourcing decisions changed.

The merchandising team also learned that late inbound arrivals damaged promotion economics by forcing markdowns near expiry.

## 6. Key Takeaways

- Treat supplier governance as a performance system, not a procurement checklist.
- Measure service-adjusted total cost, not invoice cost alone.
- Joint planning with top suppliers improves both promotion readiness and in-stock reliability.
- OTIF and quality metrics should be visible to merchandising, supply chain, and stores.
