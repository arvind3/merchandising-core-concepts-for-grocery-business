# Chapter 6: Store Operations and Execution

## Concept Explanation

**Store Operations** encompasses all activities required to run a retail store, including merchandising execution, customer service, inventory management, and daily operational tasks.

**Execution** refers to the implementation of merchandising plans at the store level, ensuring that corporate strategies are correctly translated to the sales floor.

### Key Store Operations Concepts

**Planogram Compliance**
- Adherence to planned product placement and shelf layout
- Measured through audits and photo verification
- Directly impacts sales and vendor relationships

**Front-to-Back Flow**
- Logical product movement from receiving to sales floor
- Minimizes handling and maximizes efficiency
- Critical for fresh products with limited shelf life

**Store Labor Standards**
- Task time estimates for standard operations
- Used for staffing and scheduling
- Includes: receiving, stocking, facing, cleaning, customer service

## Business Use Case

**Scenario: New Store Opening at Metro Grocery**

Metro Grocery is opening a new 45,000 sq ft store with a grand opening in 4 weeks.

### Week 1: Staffing and Training

**Store Manager Hired:**
- 1 Store Manager
- 3 Assistant Managers (Front End, Grocery, Fresh)
- 15 Department Managers
- 75 Team Members (full-time and part-time)

**Training Program:**
```
Day 1-2: Company orientation, policies, systems
Day 3-4: Department-specific training
Day 5: Customer service standards
Day 6-7: Merchandising execution training
Day 8-10: System training (POS, inventory, task management)
Day 11-12: Mock operations and dry runs
Day 13: Final assessment and certification
```

### Week 2: Initial Stocking

**Receiving Process:**
- First wave of inventory arrives from DC
- 20 delivery trucks scheduled over 3 days
- Each department team verifies and stocks their products

**Quality Checks:**
- Fresh products inspected for quality and date codes
- Temperature verification for cold chain items
- Damaged products documented and returned

### Week 3: Planogram Implementation

**Reset Teams Deployed:**
- 5 reset teams (4 members each)
- Each team assigned to specific departments
- Planograms loaded on tablets for reference

**Compliance Verification:**
```
Department: Dry Grocery
Total SKUs: 8,500
Planogram Bays: 450
Compliance Target: 100%
Verification Method: Photo audit + manager walk-through
```

**Issues Found and Resolved:**
- 12 products missing from shipment 竊' DC expedite requested
- 5 shelf tags incorrect 竊' reprinted and replaced
- 3 planogram discrepancies 竊' corporate notified, corrections made

### Week 4: Final Preparation

**Soft Opening (3 days before grand opening):**
- Limited hours (10 AM - 6 PM)
- Friends and family shopping
- Staff practices real operations
- Issues logged and resolved daily

**Grand Opening Readiness Checklist:**
| Item | Status | Owner |
|------|--------|-------|
| Staffing complete | 笨... | Store Manager |
| Inventory stocked | 笨... | Dept Managers |
| Planogram compliance | 98% | Reset Lead |
| POS systems tested | 笨... | IT Support |
| Cleaning complete | 笨... | Maintenance |
| Signage installed | 笨... | Marketing |
| Safety inspection passed | 笨... | Safety Manager |

### Grand Opening Results

**Day 1 Metrics:**
- Customer count: 2,847
- Transactions: 2,103
- Average basket: $67.42
- Total sales: $141,784
- Customer satisfaction: 4.6/5.0

**Issues Encountered:**
- Front end lines backed up during peak (11 AM - 2 PM)
- Popular items sold out by 3 PM
- One POS terminal had connectivity issues

**Corrective Actions:**
- Added 2 additional checkout lanes for Day 2
- Emergency DC delivery scheduled for overnight
- IT replaced POS terminal network card

## Users Involved

### Store Leadership

**Store Manager**
- Overall store performance and P&L
- Staff management and development
- Customer satisfaction
- Compliance with corporate standards

**Assistant Store Manager**
- Supports Store Manager
- Often oversees specific areas (front end, operations)
- Acts as Manager on Duty (MOD)

### Department Management

**Department Manager (Grocery)**
- Manages grocery department
- Ensures planogram compliance
- Oversees stocking and facing
- Manages department inventory

**Department Manager (Fresh/Produce)**
- Manages fresh departments
- Ensures product quality and rotation
- Oversees preparation and merchandising
- Manages shrink and waste

**Department Manager (Front End)**
- Manages checkout operations
- Oversees customer service desk
- Manages cashier scheduling
- Handles customer issues

### Store Team

**Cashier**
- Processes customer transactions
- Provides customer service
- Maintains checkout area cleanliness
- Handles returns and exchanges

**Stock Clerk**
- Unloads delivery trucks
- Stocks shelves from backroom
- Faces products (pulls forward)
- Maintains backroom organization

**Customer Service Associate**
- Handles customer inquiries
- Processes returns and exchanges
- Manages loyalty program signups
- Resolves customer issues

**Maintenance/Custodian**
- Maintains store cleanliness
- Handles spills and safety issues
- Performs basic repairs
- Manages trash and recycling

### Corporate Support

**District Manager**
- Oversees multiple stores (8-12)
- Coaches Store Managers
- Ensures regional consistency
- Escalates issues to corporate

**Retail Operations Manager**
- Develops store operating procedures
- Creates training materials
- Leads continuous improvement
- Supports new store openings

**IT Support Specialist**
- Maintains store technology
- Troubleshoots POS and systems
- Manages network connectivity
- Supports hardware repairs

### Quality Engineering Roles

**Field Quality Auditor**
- Conducts store quality audits
- Validates process compliance
- Reports findings to corporate
- Follows up on corrective actions

**Systems Quality Engineer**
- Tests store system deployments
- Validates POS functionality

## Chapter Summary

Store operations and execution are where merchandising strategy meets reality. Effective store operations require clear processes, well-trained staff, and reliable systems. For IT and Quality Engineering professionals, store systems present unique challenges:

- Distributed environment with varying connectivity
- High-volume transaction processing
- Multiple hardware integrations (POS, scanners, scales)
- Real-time inventory accuracy requirements
- Customer-facing systems requiring high reliability
