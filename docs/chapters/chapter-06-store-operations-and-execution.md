# Chapter 6: Store Operations and Execution

Merchandising quality is validated in stores, not in planning decks. The final meter between strategy and customer experience is shelf execution: placement, stock condition, signage accuracy, and team routines.

## 1. Concept Explanation

Store execution in grocery is driven by five control loops:

1. **Planogram execution**: shelf layout, facings, and adjacency rules.
2. **Price integrity**: shelf label, POS file, and promotion sign consistency.
3. **Availability control**: shelf replenishment cadence and backroom flow.
4. **Freshness management**: rotation, date checks, markdown timing.
5. **Display productivity**: endcaps and secondary display compliance.

Most chains fail not because strategy is weak, but because execution standards are not measurable at store level.

## 2. Why It Matters to the Business

A strong central plan with weak store execution creates avoidable losses:

- Lost sales from phantom inventory or empty facings.
- Margin loss from incorrect pricing and unplanned markdowns.
- Brand erosion when stores present inconsistent customer experience.

Store operations should therefore be managed with daily and weekly cadence metrics.

## 3. Example Scenario: Planogram Compliance Recovery

A multi-format chain observed large performance variance between stores with similar demographics. Investigation showed inconsistent planogram and price execution.

### Program Design

- Introduced a weekly compliance score by department.
- Added mobile audit tooling with photo verification.
- Created a two-tier exception path: local demand variance and operational constraints.
- Linked store bonuses partially to compliance and price integrity.

### 16-Week Impact (Illustrative)

| KPI | Week 0 | Week 16 |
|---|---:|---:|
| Planogram compliance | 76% | 93% |
| Shelf price accuracy | 95.2% | 99.1% |
| OSA audit score | 91% | 96% |
| Display execution score | 68% | 88% |

## 4. Data Interpretation

The chain compared store clusters and found:

- High compliance stores outperformed peers on both sales and margin, not sales alone.
- Price accuracy errors were concentrated around promotion transition days.
- Stores with structured backroom-to-shelf routines had lower phantom inventory incidents.

Key management shift: move from monthly compliance reporting to weekly operational coaching.

## 6. Key Takeaways

- Store execution is a measurable system, not subjective craftsmanship.
- Planogram and price integrity are leading indicators of category performance.
- Weekly audit and coaching loops outperform monthly exception management.
- Layout and display strategy must align with trip mission behavior by store format.
