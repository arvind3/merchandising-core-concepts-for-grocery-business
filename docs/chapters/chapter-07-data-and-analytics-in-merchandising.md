# Chapter 7: Data and Analytics in Merchandising

The analytical maturity of a grocery retailer is visible in one question: are dashboards describing yesterday, or are decision systems improving tomorrow?

## 1. Concept Explanation

Merchandising analytics should progress through four layers:

1. **Descriptive**: what happened.
2. **Diagnostic**: why it happened.
3. **Predictive**: what is likely to happen.
4. **Prescriptive**: what action should be taken.

Critical analytical domains in grocery:

- Category performance decomposition
- Basket analytics and affinity modeling
- Price and promotion response modeling
- Forecast error and replenishment exceptions
- Store execution and compliance analytics

## 2. Why It Matters to the Business

Data investments pay off only when decisions change:

| Analytics capability | Typical business decision improved |
|---|---|
| Basket affinity | Cross-category display and bundle design |
| Elasticity models | Price changes by segment and channel |
| Promo post-event analytics | Future calendar and mechanic selection |
| OSA root cause analysis | Store process and supply fixes |

## 3. Example Scenario: Basket-Led Growth Program

A supermarket group used loyalty and transaction data to improve attachment between meal-oriented categories.

### Analytical Findings

- Pasta purchases were strongly associated with pasta sauce and grated cheese.
- Promotions on one anchor item worked best when supported by secondary placement of companion categories.
- Single-SKU discounting generated less gross profit than meal-bundle structure.

### Actions

- Created cross-category meal bundles with moderate discount depth.
- Deployed endcap-plus-aisle signage linking complementary items.
- Targeted loyalty offers to households with low attach rates.

### Results (Illustrative)

| KPI | Before | After |
|---|---:|---:|
| Average basket value | $28.40 | $30.10 |
| Attach rate (sauce with pasta) | 41% | 53% |
| Promo gross profit | 100 index | 121 index |

## 4. Data Interpretation

A naive reading would credit all gains to discounting. Controlled analysis showed the larger effect came from trip-mission orchestration and cross-category placement.

The key insight was structural: basket behavior is a network phenomenon. Optimizing one SKU at a time leaves profitable adjacency opportunities unused.

## 6. Key Takeaways

- Move analytics from report production to decision design.
- Basket-level analysis reveals cross-category profit opportunities invisible in SKU-only views.
- Promo and placement should be evaluated as coordinated levers.
- The best merchandising organizations institutionalize post-event learning loops.
